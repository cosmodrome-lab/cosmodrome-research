### Open Questions — HYPE Investigation

We publish facts and raise sharp questions about Hyperliquid (HYPE).  
We are looking for researchers, on-chain analysts, and people genuinely interested in this type of work.

**Key open questions:**

- Who is **Hyper Holdings Global LP** and what is its connection to DCG and Genesis?
- What is the real staking percentage of **Foundation nodes** and Hyperliquid Labs? Which validators do institutional entities (Grayscale and others) delegate to?
- Where exactly does the ~$438M gap between collected fees and actual buybacks go?
- Is the narrative layer (coordinated posts on Reddit, X, Telegram) organic or coordinated?
- How sustainable is the current absorption layer system (Staking + HyperLend + HypurrFi + PURR + Grayscale + OTC) as real circulating supply grows?
- Why are key supply-related decisions made via Discord announcements instead of on-chain governance?
- Was the December 2025 "burn" of 37M tokens a technical destruction or merely a political commitment by the same validators who overrode JELLY in 2 minutes?
- **How do nodes appear and disappear?** The process is highly centralized and technically complex for independent participants. The volume of tokens flowing through nodes amounts to tens of millions of dollars. We see no public evidence that nodes actually purchased these tokens on the open market. There is a strong suspicion of a centralized, founder-controlled distribution process. Are there clearly defined rules in the smart contracts for reward distribution among nodes (including privileged ones), or are all key decisions made by a closed decision-making center?

If you have data, sources, or expertise on any of these questions, open an Issue with the tag `hype-research`.
